Robot 24

In one of my classes I was teamed up with an amazing group to build a machine that was able to accomplish a set of tasks themed around basketball. Our machine was to launch two ping pong balls into a target zone, collect three other balls that were randomly placed in a set area and then "dunk" collected balls into a specific hole in an hexagonal array of holes.

We are also to adhere to a number of rules: 1) Dimensions of Robot cannot be greater than a 20 inch cube. 2) Destruction of any of the arena props is prohibited. 3) The entire run should not take more than two minutes, one minute to set up and one minute to achieve all the specified tasks Failing to follow any of these rules will result in a disqualified run

First it tested my CAD skills

Using the CAD drawing as a reference my team and I first began by manufacturing the frame, made from 1/4" thick MDF and laser cut to the desired shape. The frame was assembled like a jig-saw puzzle then secured using brackets and screws.

For launching the ping pong ball, we experimented with multiple designs: We first tried a more "cool" approach which involved shooting the ball from an air cannon. After 3D printing the parts needed, a few tests were able to prove the air cannon to be inconsistent and unreliable.

So we opted for a catapult, which uses a lever approach to launch the ping pong balls at different angles, and when the lever was printed, the catapult was an astounding success. Both the catapult and the air cannon utilized a pneumatic system to function.

By far the most challenging part of building the robot was the collect and dunk functions, and therefore the most satisfying success story. Using a funnel with strategically placed hole and specific height, did great work at collecting the balls. Then utilizing a drawer slide and a simple pulley system we able to extend the "arm" to reach the distance we want it to.

Finally, the brain of our robot was placed on top. The microcontroller controlled the motors and actuating the pneumatic system on command and made sure all the tasks were done in under a minute.

After months of hard work, planning and many iterations, I am excited to introduce to you Robot 24.

Even though we did not win first place in the competion, we still got one of the highest scores at 2020 ME2110 GT competion, and are very proud of 24 and the opportunity to bring it to life!
